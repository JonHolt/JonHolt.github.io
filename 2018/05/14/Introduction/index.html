Introduction

2018-05-14 Comments Beginning

Welcome to my blog! My name is Jonathan. The purpose of this blog is for me to get some practice with creative writing, and for you to (hopefully) get some entertainment out of it. Please read the rest of this post to get the backround on why this blog exists, and how it will operate. Or if that sounds boring to you I’ve provided this handy tl;dr.

TL;DR

  • Read the latest post of the blog, which will be on the home page.
  • Vote in the comments on what should happen next.
  • Repeat one week later.
  • Sometimes there will be a bonus story that my brother made me write.
  • Stories will be organized by category.

Before starting this blog, my brother and I decided to practice our creative writing by having weekly “story challenges” where we find a writing prompt online and write stories for them over the course of a week. At the end of the week we’re supposed to share our stories with each other and pick a new prompt. We started about a year ago, we’ve done somewhere around… four challenges, and I’ve shown my brother only one of my stories (all my fault).

This year of failure has taught me some things about myself though:

  • I’m great at outlining story ideas from start to finish.
  • I’m less great at actually writing and filling in the details of the story.
  • I’m real bad at sharing what I do write. (The AI that scans all Google Drive documents for ad ideas is my #1 fan).

So the idea of this blog is to remove the things I’m good at with creative writing (pretty much just planning), in order to focus on improving my weaknesses.

Format

Once a week, on Tuesdays, I will post a segment of a story to this blog. Over the course of the next few days, until Friday, you will have the chance to read the new post, comment what you think should happen next, and vote on other reader’s comments with which you agree. On Friday evening I will accept the comment with the most votes as the prompt for the next segment of the story. If there is a tie for top suggestion I will break the tie by picking my favorite.

When you feel like your suggestion should be the end of the story, say so in your comment. If a ending suggestion is accepted the story will end with the accompanying post.

I still intend to keep doing the occasional story challenge with my brother. To keep me honest, I will be posting those stories here under the “Story Challenge” category of this blog.

About

This is a creative writing blog by Jonathan Holt, written for practice in creative writing and your entertainment.
