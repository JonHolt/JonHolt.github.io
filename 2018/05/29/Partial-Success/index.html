Partial Success

2018-05-29 Comments Look Before You Leap

A heavy silence fell over the mission control center for approximately 1.3 seconds between the time Captain Meyer’s transponder signal disappeared from the main display, up to the collective sigh of relief when it appeared again at the exact coordinates specified. A cheer went up among the engineers monitoring the telemetry data.

“Alright, alright! Quiet down! There will be plenty of time to celebrate later.” That was Remi Ashton, the ground commander for this test flight. “Thomas, ask her for a status report.”

“Captain Meyer, we’re reading that you’ve arrived at precisely the correct coordinates. How was the trip?”

An excited hubbub persisted in the room, despite the order to quiet down. A few engineers around the edges of the room were still chatting happily about the ramifications of human faster than light travel. Discussions ranged from excitement about the future of exploration in and around the solar system, to jealousy that Captain Meyer was the first to try it.

But the conversations, the bravado, and the smiles, all began to fade quickly as the expected 2.6 second transmission time passed without a response from the Captain.

“Captain, please respond, are you alright?” Thomas was always good at keeping the strain out of his voice when he was stressed. Perhaps that’s how he got the job as capcom.

Ashton turned to one of the controllers who was monitoring telemetry. “She could be hurt, bring up the camera feed of the cockpit.”

Another 2.6 seconds passed in an absence of sound somehow even more crushing than the moment following the jump.

“We’re getting the video feed now sir.” A window popped up on the main screen of the command center. The video showed the cockpit of Captain Meyer’s vessel in perfect working order, with one exception; The captain herself was missing.

“Will somebody please tell me where the hell Captain Meyer went?”
