déjà vu

2018-06-05 Comments Look Before You Leap

“…Captain? Captain Meyer! Can you read me?”

Sarah awoke to a splitting headache. “Oh god…” she muttered as her double-vision of the capsule slowly merged into one. “What the h-”

“How’re you feeling Sarah?” the concern in Thomas’ voice was apparent. “Do we need to scrub the launch?”

“Scrub the launch? Didn’t we…” already launch? Sarah bit her tongue. Of course I haven’t launched yet, otherwise I wouldn’t be experiencing gravity. She looked out of the window of her capsule.

Blue sky.

“Repeat that last Captain, you cut out.”

“Didn’t we talk about this Thomas? This launch is too important to scrub over a little headache. I’ll be fine.” Will I?

“Understood Captain, but let us know if it gets worse.”

“I will.” She was answering herself more than Thomas.

“Then let’s finish up this preflight check and get this countdown rolling.”

Over the next five minutes Sarah fumbled awkwardly through the last of the systems checks. Not only was she having trouble focusing due to the headache, she also couldn’t shake the feeling that something wasn’t right, that she’d done this before. Then there was the fact that she couldn’t find half of the controls in the cockpit without help. She could swear they were organized differently last time. Last time? There was no last time. This is the first launch.

“I’m not sure we should proceed with the launch.” Thomas’ voice came hesitantly over the coms, “I’m recommending we postpone the launch until we can verify the pilot is capable of completing the mission.”

“It’ll be fine.” Sarah insisted, “The autopilot will be doing all the work anyway.”

“…Sarah, there is no autopilot. We took that system out when we discovered it was interfering with the warp drive in the unmanned tests.”

Silence

“Hold on Captain Meyer, we’re awaiting a medical opinion on whether you’re okay to launch.”
