Check Up

2018-06-12 Comments Look Before You Leap

“Try your best to describe to me what you’re feeling.” The doctor said while shining a light in Sarah’s eye. Sarah flinched away from the light and tried to blink the flashing after-image into submission.

“It’s just a little headache mostly, nothing a baby aspirin won’t fix.” She began, “Otherwise just a little bit of déjà vu and some minor disorientation that I’m sure is caused by the headache.”

“Look here.” the doctor directed before cruelly shining his light in her other eye. While Sarah was confident she had been in good shape to fly before this exam, she wasn’t so sure that would be the case when this doctor was finished blinding her. The doctor made a note on his clipboard. “Can you elaborate on the disorientation?”

“It’s not that bad really,” Sarah said, once again blinking frantically to clear her vision. “It just took me a bit longer than normal to find some of the controls. It’s like the configuration of the control panel was… slightly different from what I’m used to. Also, I can’t get over the feeling that I’ve been through this before.”

The doctor had strapped an inflatable cuff around her arm and was in the process of pumping it up to check her blood pressure. “What, this exam?” If he was joking that would be the first hint of bedside manner had seen in the man, though his face showed no hint of a joke.

“No. This is all new.” Something in Sarah’s voice made it clear she’d rather it weren’t happening at all. She caught herself thinking back on the launch when she didn’t have to get a medical exam; or forward, or… laterally? She wasn’t really sure. She’d rather be experiencing that glorious launch that she remembered so clearly, despite the fact that she was certain now that it had never happened. “I mean the launch. I remember launching, reaching orbit, making a joke about a file cabinet, and even punching in the coordinates for a quick jump to the moon and back.”

“You remember that far ahead?” this actually got the doctor to look up from his clipboard. “That’s a bit unusual for déjà vu, it’s usually just that you remember what you’re currently doing as being familiar. It’s never actually a ‘memory’ of the future.” He stopped to think for a few silent seconds. “How was your trip to the moon then, the one you remember?”

“Well I didn’t make it that far…” Sarah drifted off trying to remember beyond the first jump. “I remember punching in the coordinates, and then counting down, and then I was here about to take off again only with a splitting headache.”

“I thought you said it was nothing a baby aspirin wouldn’t fix.”

“Right, yeah. That’s what I meant.” Sarah didn’t even look a little guilty about getting caught in her lie.

“Uh huh….” The doctor was back to his clipboard. At that moment, there was a knock at the exam room’s door. The doctor set down his clipboard and opened the door a crack. “What is it?”

“We need Captain Meyer in the control room. We’re picking up a transponder signal from… something in orbit around the moon. It’s the signal from her ship.”
