Look Before You Leap

2018-05-22 Comments Look Before You Leap

Captain Sarah Meyer took a moment to relish the familiar feeling of weightlessness as the thrust cut out at the end of her orbital insertion burn. She smiled down at the brilliant blue Earth below her.

“I gotta say Houston,” she began with a hint of mischief in her voice, “this thing flies like a file cabinet.”

“I’d like to see you fit humanity’s first ever hyperdrive into a file ca-”

“Would the engineers please leave capsule communication to the capsule communicator?” Thomas was on capcom today. “I’m sorry to hear that sitting in the pilot seat while autopilot took you into orbit was too much strain on your piloting skills Sarah. What’s your status?”

“Green across the board Houston” Sarah replied, “I’m reading that I’ve reached the target orbit, and the drive status is ready. Can you confirm?”

“Yes we’re seeing the same readings down here. Remember now, no new variables. We’re just running the same test that ten drones have already done before you. I want the only change here to be the fact that you’re driving. Jump to the programmed coordinates near the moon and report back when you get there, got it?”

“Understood Houston.” The first human being to ever jump to hyperspace grinned like a child in an ice cream shop as she input her destination into the jump computer and placed her thumb on the button to activate the hyperspace engine. “Jumping in 5, 4, 3, 2, 1.”
